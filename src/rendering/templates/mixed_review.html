Why mixed practice works

Interleaved (mixed) practice — where you encounter different exercise types and topics in unpredictable order — outperforms blocked practice by a significant margin. It feels harder in the moment, but the added difficulty of switching between contexts forces deeper processing and better discrimination between similar concepts. Different exercise types (quizzes, ordering, matching, fill-in-the-blank) engage different cognitive pathways, creating richer and more flexible knowledge.

Based on: Brunmair & Richter (2019) meta-analysis, effect size g = 0.42; Kornell & Bjork (2008); Roediger & Karpicke (2006) testing effect; Bjork & Bjork (2011) desirable difficulties framework.
