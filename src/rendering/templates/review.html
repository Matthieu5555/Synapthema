Why spaced review works

Spaced repetition is one of the most proven techniques in learning science. By reviewing material just as you're about to forget it, you strengthen long-term memory far more effectively than re-reading or cramming. The FSRS algorithm schedules each item based on your personal performance — items you find difficult come back sooner, easy ones are spaced further apart.

Based on: Cepeda et al. (2006) meta-analysis of 184 studies; Rowland (2014) testing effect meta-analysis showing g = 0.50 effect size.
