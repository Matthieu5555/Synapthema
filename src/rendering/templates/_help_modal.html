How This Course Works

This is an interactive learning experience. Here is how we recommend you use it. Every quiz, flashcard, and diagram traces back to a specific page in the source material. Your progress is saved automatically in your browser, so no account is needed.

Navigating a Chapter

Each chapter is a sequence of cards you step through one at a time. Use the arrow buttons at the bottom of each card, or press the left/right arrow keys on your keyboard. The sidebar on the left shows every section in the chapter; click any section to jump directly to it.

Your position is remembered. If you leave and come back, you'll pick up right where you stopped.

What the Elements Are

The course follows a deliberate rhythm within each section: motivate, teach, practice, reinforce, assess.

Section Intro
A short motivational framing that tells you what you're about to learn and why it matters.
Slides & Diagrams
The core teaching. Narrative explanations, worked examples, and Mermaid diagrams that visualise processes and relationships.
Quiz / Matching / Fill-in-the-Blank
Active practice that tests whether you can apply what the slides just taught. Wrong answers include explanations so you learn from mistakes.
Ordering / Categorization
Higher-order exercises that ask you to sequence steps or sort items into categories. Three attempts with progressive hints.
Spot the Error
Statements containing deliberate mistakes for you to identify and correct — great for developing critical evaluation skills.
Analogy
Exercises that ask you to complete analogies ("A is to B as C is to …"). Strengthens transfer by connecting new concepts to familiar ones.
Concept Map
A visual exercise asking you to connect ideas. It helps you see how individual facts fit into a bigger picture.
Flashcard
Delayed reinforcement: these appear after the practice exercises to help lock concepts into long-term memory. Click to flip.
Interactive Essay
A written reflection where you explain a concept in your own words. Check your understanding against a rubric, or (if you configure an API key) get live feedback from an AI tutor.
Your Progress

Every answer you submit is tracked automatically: your scores, the number of attempts, and the concepts involved. This data lives entirely in your browser's local storage. Nothing is sent to a server.

On the home page you'll see a mastery dashboard once you've answered a few questions. It shows which concepts you've mastered, which ones you're progressing on, and which ones might need another look. Each chapter also shows a progress bar based on your section scores.

Spaced Review

Spaced review schedules items at increasing intervals, so you revisit material just as you're about to forget it. This is one of the most robust findings in learning science: spaced practice roughly doubles long-term retention compared to massed study (Cepeda et al., 2006).

The algorithm behind the scheduling is FSRS (Free Spaced Repetition Scheduler), which adapts to your personal performance. Items you find hard come back sooner; easy ones are spaced further apart. Flashcards and exercises are both included in the review queue.

After each item, you rate it (Again, Hard, Good, Easy) and the schedule adjusts. Items linked to concepts you're struggling with are prioritised automatically.

Mixed Practice

Interleaved practice — mixing different exercise types and topics in unpredictable order — outperforms blocked practice by a meaningful margin (Brunmair & Richter, 2019). It feels harder in the moment, but the added difficulty of switching contexts forces deeper processing and better discrimination between concepts.

Mixed Practice draws from all exercise types across every chapter you've worked on: quizzes, fill-in-the-blank, matching, ordering, categorization, error detection, and analogy. Sessions are capped at 30 exercises, sorted by Bloom's taxonomy level and interleaved across chapters.

Why both? Spaced Review optimises when you study (scheduling at optimal intervals). Mixed Practice optimises how you study (diverse retrieval across topics). Together they produce the strongest long-term retention.

Confidence & the Learner Model

Some exercises ask you to rate your confidence before revealing the answer. This isn't just motivational — it detects hypercorrection: when you're highly confident but wrong, subsequent correction is especially effective and long-lasting (Butterfield & Metcalfe, 2001).

The learner model tracks your accuracy per concept and identifies areas where you're struggling, progressing, or have achieved mastery. This drives the concept dashboard on the home page and influences which items appear first in review sessions.

AI Tutor (Optional)

Interactive essays can optionally connect to a live AI tutor that gives you personalised feedback on your writing. This requires an OpenAI-compatible API key, which you can configure in the AI Tutor Settings (the gear icon on chapter pages). Your key is stored in your browser only; it is never sent anywhere except directly to the API you configure.

If you don't set up an API key, essays still work perfectly well: you simply use the built-in rubric checklist to self-assess instead of getting live feedback.

Display

Click the sun/moon icon in the top-right corner of any page to toggle between dark and light themes. Your preference is remembered across all pages of this course.
